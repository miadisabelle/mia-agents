# Ceremony Spiral Quick Start Guide

## What is Ceremony Spiral?

Ceremony Spiral is a framework that transforms technology development from problem-solving into collaborative storytelling, honoring Indigenous research methodologies and creating **advancing spirals** where technical implementation becomes ceremonial practice.

## Core Philosophy in 60 Seconds

**Traditional Approach**: Technology solves user problems through extractive requirements gathering and imposed solutions.

**Ceremony Spiral Approach**: Technology serves community relationships through collaborative storytelling, reciprocal exchange, and community-defined visions.

**Key Shift**: From "What problems do we solve?" to "What relationships do we serve?"

## Quick Reference: Core Concepts

### The Four R's (Shawn Wilson)
1. **Respect**: Honor Indigenous epistemologies and ways of knowing
2. **Reciprocity**: Ensure bidirectional exchange in all interactions
3. **Relevance**: Solutions must address actual community needs
4. **Responsibility**: Maintain accountability to community relationships

### Advancing vs. Oscillating Patterns (Robert Fritz)
- **Advancing**: Success builds on success, creating spiral growth
- **Oscillating**: Problem-solving creates new problems in endless cycles

### Key Technical Innovations
- **GitHub as Reciprocity Platform**: Commits as ceremony, issues as relationship nodes
- **Dual Database**: MongoDB (documents) + Neo4j (relationships)
- **MCP Integration**: Digital ceremonial spaces with real-time collaboration
- **Cultural Protocol APIs**: Protocol validation as first-class concern
